# AI Risk Classification Evaluation Results

## Objective
The objective of this project was to evaluate how well a large language model (LLM) can classify AI use cases into risk categories such as High Risk, Medium Risk, Low Risk, and Minimal Risk.

## Method
A dataset of AI use cases was created and classified by the LLM using a consistent prompt. The AI-generated risk classifications and explanations were then evaluated to determine whether the classifications were correct, reasonable, or incorrect based on AI risk considerations and risk-based AI frameworks.

## Findings
The AI model generally performed well when classifying AI use cases that involved employment, finance, and law enforcement, which are typically considered higher-risk applications. The model also correctly classified lower-risk use cases such as translation and document summarization.

Some classifications were reasonable but could vary depending on context and implementation details. This demonstrates that AI risk classification often requires human judgment and contextual understanding.

## Conclusion
LLMs can assist with AI risk classification and governance workflows, but human oversight is necessary to ensure consistent and appropriate risk classification decisions. This project demonstrates the importance of human-in-the-loop evaluation when using AI systems for governance, risk, and policy-related decision support.
