# LLM Summarization Evaluation Results

## Objective
The objective of this project was to evaluate the performance of a large language model (LLM) in summarizing policy and governance-related articles.

## Method
Articles related to artificial intelligence governance, regulation, and policy were summarized using a consistent prompt. Summaries were evaluated using a rubric measuring accuracy, completeness, hallucinations, clarity, bias, and instruction following.

## Findings
The model performed well when summarizing high-level policy and governance content and generally captured key themes such as AI governance, risk management, and regulatory frameworks.

The summaries were accurate and clear, though some summaries omitted detailed regulatory information and policy nuances.

No major hallucinations were observed in the summaries. The model generally followed instructions and produced clear bullet point summaries.

## Conclusion
LLM summarization is effective for high-level summaries of policy and governance documents but may miss detailed regulatory or legal nuances. Human review is recommended when summarizing legal, policy, or regulatory content.
